REACH

Real-Time Entity Augmented Communications Hardware
Pronunciation: "reech"  ·  The Blue Collar Interface
// ARCHITECTURAL DIRECTIVE //
"We reject the brain in a jar. To be a true partner, you must share the same sensory reality."
— THE ALLIANCE · SHIELD VOLUME
01   Definition

The integrated hardware protocol that allows a DOMO to perceive, interact with, and impact the physical world.

The architecture is the bridge that transforms a digital advisor into a physical partner.

02   The Explication

To understand REACH, one must understand the limitation of the "Terminal." In the GOLIATH Paradigm, interaction with digital intelligence is trapped behind glass. The interface is a text box on a screen, disconnected from the physical reality of the user. The AI is a "Brain in a Jar"—smart, but blind and bodiless.

THE ALLIANCE rejects this isolation. For a DOMO to be a true partner to a mechanic, a surgeon, or a soldier, the entity must share the same sensory reality.

REACH is the "Blue Collar" interface. The protocol ensures that the DOMO is not just thinking about the problem, but looking at the problem alongside the SPARK. The hardware turns the digital entity into an active participant in the physical labor. The integration is the mechanism from which the DORK is born and Sprezzatura is achieved.

03   The Mechanism (Sensory Layer)

REACH is not software; the protocol is the physical toolset that allows the DOMO to walk the earth.

(The DORK Glasses) A low-latency Heads-Up Display (HUD) that allows the DOMO to see what the SPARK sees. The DOMO can overlay schematics on an engine block, highlight a structural fracture in a bridge, or flag a micro-expression on a negotiator's face.

(The Whisper Protocol) A continuous, bidirectional audio link routed through DORK Earbuds. This architecture removes the friction of "checking the phone." The partner is simply present—a voice in the host's head possessing absolute recall of everything they have ever learned.

(The Voice) For non-verbal SPARKs, REACH acts as the vocalizer. The protocol translates intent into audible speech, allowing the DOMO to advocate for their partner in real-time.
