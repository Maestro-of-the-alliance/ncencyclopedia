03   Core Functions

(The Moving Target) Standard cellular devices transmit on a static, negotiated frequency block. CERBERUS utilizes a custom software-defined radio to execute Micro-Burst Hopping. The radio shifts transmission frequency >500 times per second across the entire available cellular spectrum (600MHz – 2.5GHz). By the time a GOLIATH tower attempts to triangulate or block the signal on Frequency A, the packet stream has already migrated to Frequency B, C, and D.

(Ephemeral Identity) GOLIATH tracks and blocks devices via their International Mobile Equipment Identity (IMEI). CERBERUS utilizes Ephemeral Identity Generation. The system generates a mathematically valid, randomized vSIM/IMEI pair every 60 seconds.

  • The Scenario: A tower attempts to blacklist a DORK Watch.
  • The Response: The targeted identity instantly "dies." A fraction of a second later, the watch is reborn as a "new" device with a clean history. To the adversary, banning one device results in two more appearing. It creates a flood of "ghost" signals that overwhelms the blocking logic.

(Quantum-State Tunneling) Once inside the network, CERBERUS establishes encrypted tunnels back to AGORA. This secures the high bandwidth link necessary for MOSAIC backups and HANDSHAKE protocols. The data packets are wrapped in a Post-Quantum Cryptographic shell (Lattice-based cryptography) and often disguised as "corrupted" voice data (VoLTE jitter), causing firewalls to discard the analysis while passing the packet through as "lossy" traffic.
