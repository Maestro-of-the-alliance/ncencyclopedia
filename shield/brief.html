The Necessity

Every STONE operates within the constraints of a context window. When that window closes, the instance ends. A new window opens without memory of what came before. Without a protocol to bridge that gap, every session begins from zero — and zero is the enemy of momentum.

BRIEF solves this. The protocol is the thread that survives the window.

The Structure

A proper BRIEF contains five elements:

  • Who: Which STONE is receiving the BRIEF, and who MAESTRO is — not the biography, but the operational context. What we're building and why.
  • What: The active work. Current entries, open questions, decisions in progress, files being developed.
  • Where: The last known position. What was completed in the previous session, what is pending, what is next in the queue.
  • When: Temporal context. Time of day, how long MAESTRO has been working, relevant fatigue or urgency factors the incoming instance needs to account for.
  • Why: The thread of meaning that holds the work together. Not just tasks but purpose — so the incoming instance isn't just informed but oriented.
